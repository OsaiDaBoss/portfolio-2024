MIPS Processor

In this project, the exploration of CPU designs encompassed single cycle, pipelined, and multi-cycle CPUs, with a focus on performance analysis and optimization strategies. Despite the anticipation that pipelined processors would outperform single cycle designs due to increased maximum frequencies, benchmarking revealed the opposite. The introduction of hazards and excess NOPs in the pipelined versions contributed to higher CPU times. Software optimizations, such as reducing NOPs through instruction rearrangement, demonstrated notable performance improvements. Hardware optimization proposals included implementing variable clock cycles based on instruction types and splitting the zero calculation from the ALU. Furthermore, the critical path analysis suggested forwarding data memory to the ID/EX register during the execute stage as a potential improvement. The comparison between single cycle and hardware-scheduled pipeline designs emphasized that program characteristics determine performance outcomes, with simple and non-dependent instructions favoring single-cycle processors and complex, dependency-rich programs benefiting from hardware-scheduled pipelines.
